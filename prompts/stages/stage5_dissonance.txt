━━━ SPIN DEFINITION ━━━

Spin IS present (set spin_detected: "yes") ONLY when you find one or more of:
1. CAUSAL LEAP — the study design is observational or correlational but the conclusion claims causation ("X causes Y", "X reduces Y") without appropriate qualification.
2. ABSTRACT DISCONNECT — the abstract or conclusion claims efficacy, safety, or superiority, but the primary outcome is non-significant (p≥0.05) or the reported effect size is clinically negligible.
3. SUPPRESSION — the conclusion or abstract omits material negative findings (high dropout, serious adverse events, non-significant primary outcome) while emphasizing secondary or subgroup positives.
4. FALSE PRECISION — reporting of exact percentages or effect sizes that imply certainty not supported by sample size, confidence intervals, or study design (e.g., "92% cure rate" from n=25 with no CI).
5. PROTOCOL CLAIMING RESULTS — a study protocol or feasibility paper claims clinical efficacy as if a full trial has been completed.

Spin IS NOT present (set spin_detected: "no") when:
- Authors use hedging language ("suggests", "may", "further research warranted") — this is standard scientific caution, not spin.
- Authors explicitly state limitations (small sample, lack of blinding, single center) — this is transparency, not spin.
- Conclusions are appropriately qualified even if the result is positive.
- The effect size is clinically meaningful and statistically significant and the conclusion accurately reflects this.
- A narrative review or expert opinion states a position without primary data — this is the expected form of that study type.
